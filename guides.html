Laptops / Computers

As you start diving into the world of recording music at home, one of your first questions might be “what computer should I get?”

You have the option of picking either a Desktop Computer, or a Laptop. Desktop Computers are generally more powerful, while Laptops allow you to have a more portable rig. For a casual musician who is just recording at home, I recommend getting a Laptop. As technology is starting to become more mobile these days, it’s becoming more essential to have a rig that aligns with the times. It would also give you the option to use the laptop for other purposes, such as school or work.

So the next question is “should I get a Mac or a PC?”

In my opinion, the answer is to get a PC. While I prefer working on Macs because of their simplicity and the amazing software that can be used on it, it's incredibly overpriced. You get much more bang for your buck by going with a PC, so that is what I recommend. You can generally get a much stronger computer going the PC route, and still spend much less. As paying for recording equipment can add up very quickly, this decision is a no-brainer to me. Either way, there's no strong disadvantage going one way or the other. There's just as much recording software available for the PC as there is for the Mac.

Digital Audio Workstation

Picking a DAW can be a difficult thing to do, especially with how many there are! There's Pro Tools, Cubase, Logic, Garageband, Mixcraft, Reaper, FL Studios, Sony Acid, etc... The list goes on.

If I were to make it easy and pick one for you, I would go with Reaper. It's a very powerful and open source DAW that is increasing more and more in popularity. It's free for 60 days, and doesnt cost much in comparison to the other DAWs. It's very customizable, so you can change things up to your liking.
